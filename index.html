What is accessibility testing?

testing designed to determine whether individuals with disabilities will be able to use the system in question

Who is it for?

"About 56.7 million people — 19 percent of the population — had a disability in 2010, according to a broad definition of disability, with more than half of them reporting the disability was severe, according to a comprehensive report on this population released today by the U.S. Census Bureau."
2010 Census Bureau, "Nearly 1 in 5 People Have a Disability in the U.S."

4 categories of disabilities

Visual

Auditory

Motor

Cognitive

  • Visual - Blindness, Color Blindness, Cataracts, Blocked Visual Field
  • Changing text size, screen ready accessibility, color contrast
  • Auditory - Deafness, Hardness of Hearing
  • Video Captions, Volume Controls, Audio Indicators (web Audio api)
  • Motor - Amputation, Arthritis, Reduced Dexterity
  • Full Keyboard Support, Time Restrictions, Navigation Mechanisms
  • Cognitive - Learning Disabilities, ASD, Memory Impairments
  • Clear Structure, Consistency, Predictability, Avoid distractings content

What does it mean to be accessible?

Web Content Accessibility Guidelines

Perceivable

Operable

Understandable

Robust

WCAG 2.0 Dec. 2008
WCAG 1.0 May 1995

  • Information and user interface components must be presentable to users in ways they can perceive
  • User interface components and navigation must be operable
  • Information and the operation of user interface must be understandable
  • Content must be robust enough that it can be interpreted reliably by a wide variety of user agents
